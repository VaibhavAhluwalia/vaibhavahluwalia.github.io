About Me

I’m Vaibhav Ahluwalia, a machine learning engineer interested in understanding how machine learning systems operate in real-world settings. I’m drawn to careful learning and sustained thinking, and to building systems that remain useful beyond initial experimentation.

My work focuses on designing, deploying, and reasoning about machine learning systems that move into sustained production use. I work across the lifecycle of ML systems—from modeling and feature design to system architecture and deployment—where performance, reliability, and practical constraints all matter.

I’m particularly interested in problems where modeling choices interact closely with data, infrastructure, and product considerations. These interactions often shape how systems evolve over time more than individual algorithms in isolation.

I spend much of my time reading and learning, and sometimes writing to clarify my thinking. I value thoughtful collaboration, careful engineering, and work that rewards long-term thinking.
